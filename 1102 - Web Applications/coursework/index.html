Linus Torvalds

Birth 1969 - Helsinki, Finland
Education University of Helsinki
Main Achievements Linux Kernel, Git

Personal Life

Linus started trying out programming at age 18 on a Commodore VIC-20. He got his own PC in 1991 whilst being a student at the University of Helsinki. This is where he started to create his own UNIX-based operating system as he was not satisfied with the supplied MS-DOS system.

His wife used to be his student. He set an assignment to compose an e-mail, and his wife completed the task and sent an e-mail asking him on a date. They have now settled in Portland, Oregon, USA, and have three daughters. The birth dates of his daughters are used as magic values for rebooting the Linux kernel.[1]

Controversies

Many people have criticized Linus for his aggressive behavior towards other developers. He has been known to use strong language towards other developers, usually when contributing to the Linux kernel. He describes himself as "not a people person"[2] and has said that he prefers to work alone. This has been acknowledged more recently by stepping down from Linux kernel development temporarily in 2018 to focus on his behavior.[3]
